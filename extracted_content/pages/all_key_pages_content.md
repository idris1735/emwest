## 15. MARKET OVERVIEW - NIGERIA (/market-overview/)

### Why Nigeria?
Nigeria is one of Sub Saharan Africa's largest economies. GDP in 2017 was estimated at USD376.4 billion. Lagos has over 21 million inhabitants and is the largest city in Africa. Lagos State's GDP is $131 billion, making it the 6th largest economy in Africa.

### Sector Overview:
Manufacturing industries bring approximately 10% of total GDP each year. Manufacturing activity is concentrated in Lagos, Port Harcourt, and Ibadan. Dominated by cement, building materials, food & beverages, tobacco, chemicals, fertilizers, wood, and textiles. 3 subsectors (food & beverage, cement, textile) account for 77% of manufacturing output.

### Performance:
Nigerian manufacturing sector has been performing well. Government incentives encouraging greater interest through cheaper locally manufactured goods, import bans, cheaper funding, discriminatory foreign exchange policies.

### Challenges:
- Power supply (most firms rely on generators)
- Physical infrastructural deficiencies
- Difficult access to credit
- Cost of imported raw materials and skilled labor

### Current Scenario:
Global supply chain disrupted. Many manufacturers experiencing acute shortage of raw materials and intermediate inputs.

### Future Post-COVID19:
- Automation will be key component
- More elastic workforce
- Digitally enabled workplace
- Differentiated supply chains
- More digital channels and E-commerce platforms

### Job Opportunities:
Growing demand for engineers, automation experts, digital specialists, E-commerce executives, and supply chain experts.
